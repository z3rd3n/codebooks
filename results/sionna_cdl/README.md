# Codebooks vs. an ML stand-in on realistic 38.901 CDL channels

The same five-scheme comparison as `scripts/compare_schemes.py`, but scored on
**Sionna 3GPP TR 38.901 CDL** drops instead of the synthetic random-ray channel.
Schemes: R15 Type I, R15 Type II (L=3), R16 eType II (combo 4), R17 FeType II PS
(combo 5, evaluated through the DFT PEB — its port-selection deployment model),
and `OracleSVDScheme` (8-bit quantized dominant eigenvector — an ML stand-in).

Array `(16,1)` dual-pol → P = 32 ports, N3 = 8 PMI units, n_rx = 2.
Generated by `scripts/sionna_cdl_compare.py` (default: CDL-C, 3 km/h, 80 drops,
seed 0). Every scheme is scored on the **same frozen bank** of CDL drops
(`SionnaCDLChannel.generate` is TF-driven and ignores the NumPy seed, so the
bank is pre-generated once and replayed — paired and reproducible). Each figure
ships with a `.json` of the exact plotted numbers.

## Figures

### `se_vs_snr.png` — SU spectral efficiency vs SNR (CDL-C)
SE of the reported precoder vs the per-subband eigen-beamforming upper bound,
rank 1, SNR −5…30 dB. The Type II family, R17, and the oracle sit in a tight
band just under the bound; **Type I trails by ~0.6 bits/s/Hz at 10 dB** (8.76
bound → 7.89 Type I → 8.65 oracle) because its single co-phased DFT beam cannot
match CDL-C's spread of clusters.

### `summary.png` — SGCS, feedback bits, SE@{0,10,20} dB (CDL-C)
The console table as bar charts. SGCS climbs Type I (0.50) → Type II (0.82–0.83)
→ R17 (0.89) → oracle (0.93). Overhead is on a **log axis**: Type I 22 bits,
R16 88, R15 149, R17 162, and the oracle ≈ 2048 (= N3·P·8) — two orders of
magnitude more, the price of unquantized per-element feedback. The SE bars show
how that fidelity gap shrinks in absolute rate terms as SNR rises.

### `cdl_models.png` — SGCS across CDL-A…E @ 10 dB
Per-scheme subspace alignment from sparse NLOS (CDL-A/B/C) to dominant LoS
(CDL-D/E). Two trends stand out:
- **Type I is channel-sensitive**: weakest in the rich NLOS models (0.30 on
  CDL-B, 0.50 on CDL-C) but jumps to 0.85–0.87 on the near-LoS CDL-D/E, where a
  single DFT beam already captures most of the energy.
- **The oracle is flat at ≈0.93 across all five models** — being unconstrained,
  it is essentially channel-agnostic, which sets the headroom the codebooks (and
  a future learned scheme) are chasing under each propagation condition.
  The Type II / R17 codebooks track between these two, strongest where the
  channel is sparse enough for their L beams to span the dominant subspace.

## Reproduce

```
.venv/bin/python scripts/sionna_cdl_compare.py            # CDL-C, 80 drops
.venv/bin/python scripts/sionna_cdl_compare.py --fast     # smoke test (8 drops)
.venv/bin/python scripts/sionna_cdl_compare.py --model A --drops 120 --seed 1
```

Requires the optional `sionna` extra (`pip install -e ".[sionna]"`). Not part of
the NumPy-only `make_all_figures.py` gallery (named outside its `fig_*.py` glob).
