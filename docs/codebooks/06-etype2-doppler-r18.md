# Chapter 6 — R18 eType II Doppler codebook (predicted PMI)

* **Spec:** TS 38.214 **§5.2.2.2.10** — *"Enhanced Type II codebook for
  predicted PMI"* (Release-18, "i00"). Verified: heading at line 12411 of
  [38214-i00.md](../../specs/38214-i00.md); param table is **Table 5.2.2.2.10-1**;
  reconstruction is **Table 5.2.2.2.10-2**.
* **Code:** [etype2_r18.py](../../src/nr_csi/codebooks/etype2_r18.py) — class
  `R18Type2Codebook`
* **`codebookType`:** `typeII-Doppler-r18`
* **Ports:** 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32 ($P_{CSI\text{-}RS} = 2 N_1 N_2$); $(N_1,N_2)$
  from `n1-n2-codebookSubsetRestriction-Doppler-r18` per Table 5.2.2.2.1-2.
* **Ranks:** 1–4 ($\upsilon \le 4$; the UE shall not report $\upsilon > 4$).
* **Compression:** spatial ($L$ beams) + delay ($M_v$ taps) + **temporal/Doppler
  ($Q$ shifts over $N_4$ slot intervals)** — a third DFT axis on top of R16.
* **Prereq:** [Chapter 4 — eType II R16](04-etype2-r16.md). R18 *is* R16 with one
  extra compression axis; at $N_4 = 1$ it degenerates **exactly** to R16
  (§5.2.2.2.5).

R16 compresses across **frequency**. R18 additionally compresses across **time**:
on a mobile channel the precoder evolves smoothly from slot to slot (it is the
response of a few **Doppler shifts**). So instead of sending a fresh report every
slot, the UE sends *one* report that the gNB uses to **predict** the precoder for
$N_4$ future slot intervals. The temporal axis is just another oversampled DFT,
handled identically to the frequency axis.

> **Sibling clause.** §5.2.2.2.11 *"Further enhanced Type II port selection
> codebook for predicted PMI"* (`typeII-Doppler-PortSelection-r18`) is the
> port-selection counterpart of this clause. It is a **separate** codebook
> (`R18PredictedPortSelectionCodebook`) and is *not* covered by `R18Type2Codebook`;
> this chapter is the **regular** (SD-DFT-beam) Doppler codebook only.

---

## 1. The reconstruction formula (Table 5.2.2.2.10-2)

For layer $l$, subband $t = 0,\dots,N_3-1$, and **slot interval**
$\iota = 0,\dots,N_4-1$:

$$
W^{l}_{q_1,q_2,n_1,n_2,n_{3,l},n_{4,l},p^{(1)}_l,p^{(2)}_l,\varphi_l,t,\iota}
= \frac{1}{\sqrt{N_1 N_2\, \gamma_{t,\iota,l}}}
\begin{bmatrix}
\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^{L-1} v_{m_1^{(i)},m_2^{(i)}}\, p^{(1)}_{l,0}
   \sum_{f=0}^{M_\upsilon-1} y_{t,l}^{(f)}
   \sum_{\tau=0}^{Q-1} z_{\iota,l}^{(\tau)}\, p^{(2)}_{l,i,f,\tau}\, \varphi_{l,i,f,\tau} \\[10pt]
\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^{L-1} v_{m_1^{(i)},m_2^{(i)}}\, p^{(1)}_{l,1}
   \sum_{f=0}^{M_\upsilon-1} y_{t,l}^{(f)}
   \sum_{\tau=0}^{Q-1} z_{\iota,l}^{(\tau)}\, p^{(2)}_{l,i+L,f,\tau}\, \varphi_{l,i+L,f,\tau}
\end{bmatrix},
$$

with the per-interval/per-subband power normalization

$$
\gamma_{t,\iota,l} = \sum_{i=0}^{2L-1}
   \Big(p^{(1)}_{l,\lfloor i/L\rfloor}\Big)^2
   \Big|\sum_{f=0}^{M_\upsilon-1} y_{t,l}^{(f)}
        \sum_{\tau=0}^{Q-1} z_{\iota,l}^{(\tau)}\, p^{(2)}_{l,i,f,\tau}\, \varphi_{l,i,f,\tau}\Big|^2 .
$$

The full precoder for $\upsilon$ layers is the column stack scaled by
$1/\sqrt{\upsilon}$ (Table 5.2.2.2.10-2, the $1$, $\tfrac1{\sqrt2}$,
$\tfrac1{\sqrt3}$, $\tfrac12$ prefactors for $\upsilon=1,2,3,4$).

The **temporal/Doppler DFT basis** is (spec eq. for the $Q$ vectors):

$$z_{\iota,l}^{(\tau)} = e^{\,j 2\pi\, \iota\, n_{4,l}^{(\tau)}/N_4},\qquad
  \iota = 0,\dots,N_4-1,\ \ \tau = 0,1.$$

Compared with R16, each coefficient gains an inner sum over $Q$ **Doppler shifts**
$\tau$, generated by the temporal DFT exactly as the $M_v$ delay taps are
generated by the frequency DFT. The whole thing is a **Tucker product** of three
DFT factors and a small core coefficient tensor — see
[compact.py](../../src/nr_csi/codebooks/compact.py) (`tucker_r18`).

The spec sign in `time_basis`/`freq_basis`
([dft.py](../../src/nr_csi/utils/dft.py)) is $e^{+j2\pi\cdots}$, matching the
clause verbatim; the *conjugate* used by `select` to match a measured channel is a
selection-side convention, not part of the reconstruction.

## 2. Parameters: param table, $N_4$, $Q$, $K_0$, $R$

### 2.1 `paramCombination-Doppler-r18` — Table 5.2.2.2.10-1 (full transcription)

$(L, p_\upsilon, \beta)$, where $p_\upsilon$ differs by rank bucket. Stored in
[config.py](../../src/nr_csi/config.py) `R18_PARAM_COMBOS` (`R18ParamCombo`):

| index | $L$ | $p_\upsilon\ (\upsilon\in\{1,2\})$ | $p_\upsilon\ (\upsilon\in\{3,4\})$ | $\beta$ |
|------:|:---:|:----------------------------------:|:----------------------------------:|:-------:|
| 1 | 2 | 1/8 | 1/16 | 1/4 |
| 2 | 2 | 1/4 | 1/8  | 1/2 |
| 3 | 4 | 1/4 | 1/8  | 1/4 |
| 4 | 4 | 1/4 | 1/4  | 1/4 |
| 5 | 4 | 1/4 | 1/4  | 1/2 |
| 6 | 4 | 1/4 | 1/4  | 3/4 |
| 7 | 4 | 1/2 | 1/4  | 1/2 |
| 8 | 6 | 1/4 | —    | 1/2 |
| 9 | 6 | 1/4 | —    | 3/4 |

(All nine rows are present in `R18_PARAM_COMBOS`; the "—" rows 8/9 support only
$\upsilon\le2$, encoded as `p_v34 = None`.)

The number of delay taps is $M_\upsilon = \lceil p_\upsilon N_3 / R\rceil$
([config.py](../../src/nr_csi/config.py) `m_v`), and $M_1$ denotes $M_\upsilon$ at
$\upsilon=1$.

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.** The clause's
> configuration restrictions on `paramCombination-Doppler-r18` are not enforced by
> `R18Type2Codebook.__init__`: combos **4–9 forbidden when $P_{CSI\text{-}RS}=4$**;
> **8–9 forbidden when $P_{CSI\text{-}RS}<32$**; **8–9 forbidden when any
> $r_i=1$ for $i>1$** in the RI-restriction; **8–9 forbidden when $R=2$**. The code
> accepts any of `1..9` regardless of ports, $R$, or RI restriction.

### 2.2 $N_4$ — the prediction window

**$N_4 \in \{1, 2, 4, 8\}$** (higher-layer parameter `N4`) — the PMI indicates
$N_3$ precoder matrices for each of the $N_4$ **consecutive slot intervals** of
duration $d$ slots (slot intervals defined in §5.2.1.4.2). The constructor
rejects any other value.

### 2.3 $Q$ — number of Doppler shifts per layer

The precoder is built from $L + M_\upsilon + Q$ vectors, where **$Q = 2$**
(spec, fixed). In code `self.Q = 1 if N4 == 1 else 2`: for $N_4=1$ the codebook
falls back to R16 (§5.2.2.2.5), where there is no temporal axis ($Q$ effectively 1
and $z\equiv1$). For $N_4\in\{2,4,8\}$, $Q=2$.

The two shifts per layer are $n_{4,l}=[n_{4,l}^{(0)}\ n_{4,l}^{(1)}]$ with
$n_{4,l}^{(\tau)}\in\{0,\dots,N_4-1\}$, assigned so $n_{4,l}^{(\tau)}$ increases
with $\tau$. The reference $n_{4,l}^{(0)} = 0$ is fixed; the **nonzero offset** to
$n_{4,l}^{(1)}$ is reported by $i_{1,10,l}\in\{0,\dots,N_4-2\}$, with offset value
$1\mapsto$ index $0$ (increasing order). In code:
$n_4^{(1)} = i_{1,10,l}+1$ (`shifts()` returns `[0, i110[li]+1]`).

* **$N_4 = 2$:** $i_{1,10,l}$ is **not reported** (only one possible nonzero
  offset). Code: `overhead_bits` / `serialize` write 0 bits; `validate_r18`
  requires `0 <= off < N4-1` which is `[0,1)` ⇒ offset fixed to 0.

### 2.4 Coefficient budget $K_0$ and the $2K_0$ cap

$$\boxed{\,K_0 = \big\lceil 2\,\beta\, L\, M_1\, Q \big\rceil\,}\qquad
  (\text{code: }`K0`,\ M_1 = m\_v(p_1,N_3,R))$$

Per-layer nonzero count
$K_l^{NZ}=\sum_{\tau=0}^{Q-1}\sum_{i=0}^{2L-1}\sum_{f=0}^{M_\upsilon-1}
k_{l,i,f,\tau}^{(3)} \le K_0$, and the **total** across layers
$K^{NZ}=\sum_l K_l^{NZ} \le 2K_0$ (enforced by `_enforce_total_budget`).

> **The $Q$ factor in $K_0$ is spec-faithful, not a bug (erratum 7 in the repo
> README).** On a *static* channel the second Doppler bin is empty, so its share
> of the budget is donated to the DC ($\tau{=}0$) bin. Hence at matched
> $(L, p_\upsilon, \beta)$, R18 on a static channel is **at least as accurate as
> R16** (with nothing to predict) — at strictly larger overhead. Expect R18 ≥ R16
> in static comparisons.

### 2.5 $R$ and $N_3$

$R\in\{1,2\}$ comes from `numberOfPMI-SubbandsPerCQI-Subband-Doppler-r18`; $R$ and
$N_3$ are defined as in §5.2.2.2.5 (R16). The constructor takes `R` and uses it in
$M_\upsilon$. (Note the spec restriction "8–9 forbidden when $R=2$" is **[not
implemented]** — see §2.1.)

## 3. The PMI structure ($i_1$, $i_2$)

For $N_4\in\{2,4,8\}$ the PMI splits as (clause $i_1/i_2$ matrices, per layer):

* **$i_1$** (wideband): $i_{1,1}, i_{1,2}$ (spatial group/beams), $i_{1,5}$
  ($M_{\text{initial}}$, only for $N_3>19$), and per layer
  $i_{1,6,l}$ (delay-tap combination), $i_{1,7,l}$ (bitmap),
  $i_{1,8,l}$ (strongest indicator), $i_{1,10,l}$ (second-shift offset).
* **$i_2$** (per subband-set): per layer $i_{2,3,l}$ (reference amplitudes),
  $i_{2,4,l}$ (differential amplitudes), $i_{2,5,l}$ (phases).

For $N_4=1$ the PMI is exactly the R16 $i_1/i_2$ (no $i_{1,10}$; $Q=1$).

```python
@dataclass
class R18Type2PMI:
    rank: int
    q1, q2, i12          # spatial group (q1,q2 = i1,1/i1,2) + beams (i1,2 = i12)
    i15: int | None      # M_initial  (N3 > 19)
    i16: list[int]       # per-layer delay-tap combination  (i1,6,l)
    i110: list[int]      # per-layer second Doppler-shift offset  (i1,10,l = n4^(1)-1)
    i17: np.ndarray      # bitmap (v, Q, Mv, 2L), bool       (i1,7,l)
    i18: list[int]       # strongest indicator, dual mode    (i1,8,l)
    k1:  np.ndarray      # (v, 2)         reference amplitudes  (i2,3,l: k^(1) in {1..15})
    k2:  np.ndarray      # (v, Q, Mv, 2L) differential amps     (i2,4,l: k^(2) in {0..7})
    c:   np.ndarray      # (v, Q, Mv, 2L) 16-PSK phases         (i2,5,l: c   in {0..15})
```

The only structural additions over R16 are the **Doppler dimension $Q$** on the
coefficient tensors/bitmap and the per-layer shift offset $i_{1,10,l}$. Note the
code stores `k2`/`c`/`i17` in axis order `(v, Q, Mv, 2L)` (i.e. $(\tau,f,i)$),
the transpose of the spec's $i_{2,4,l,\tau}=[k^{(2)}_{l,f,\tau}]$ nesting; the
reconstruction transposes back to $(2L,M_v,Q)$ in `_layer_coefficients`.

### 3.1 Amplitudes, phases, and 16-PSK (spec mappings)

* **Reference amplitude** $i_{2,3,l}=[k^{(1)}_{l,0}\ k^{(1)}_{l,1}]$,
  $k^{(1)}_{l,p}\in\{1,\dots,15\}$, mapped to $p^{(1)}_{l,p}$ by Table 5.2.2.2.5-2
  (`qt.R16_REF_AMP`). Two values, one per polarization.
* **Differential amplitude** $i_{2,4,l}$:
  $k^{(2)}_{l,i,f,\tau}\in\{0,\dots,7\}$ (3 bits), mapped by Table 5.2.2.2.5-3
  (`qt.R16_DIFF_AMP`).
* **Phase** $i_{2,5,l}$: $c_{l,i,f,\tau}\in\{0,\dots,15\}$ (4 bits), with
  $\varphi_{l,i,f,\tau}=e^{j2\pi c_{l,i,f,\tau}/16}$ — i.e. **16-PSK**
  (`N_PSK = 16`, `qt.phase_value`).

For coefficients with bitmap bit $k^{(3)}_{l,i,f,\tau}=0$, both amplitude and phase
are set to zero ($p^{(2)}=0,\varphi=0$). Same convention as R16, now over the
extra $\tau$ axis.

---

## 4. The bitmap $i_{1,7,l}$ over $(Q, M_v, 2L)$

$i_{1,7,l}=[i_{1,7,l,0}\ i_{1,7,l,1}]$, one sub-bitmap per shift $\tau$, each of
shape $(M_\upsilon, 2L)$, with entries $k^{(3)}_{l,i,f,\tau}\in\{0,1\}$. Total size
per layer $2L\,M_\upsilon\,Q$. In code the bitmap is `pmi.i17[li]` of shape
`(Q, Mv, 2L)`. The indices of $i_{2,4,l}$, $i_{2,5,l}$ and $i_{1,7,l}$ are
associated to the $Q$ codebook indices in $n_{4,l}$.

**Delay remapping (shared with R16).** The strongest coefficient's tap is remapped
to $f^\star_l=0$: $n_{3,l}^{(f)}\!=(n_{3,l}^{(f)}-n_{3,l}^{(f^\star_l)})\bmod N_3$
and $f=(f-f^\star_l)\bmod M_\upsilon$, so after remapping the strongest sits at
$f=0$. (Code does the cyclic `np.roll(..., -n_star, axis=2)` on the tap axis before
selecting taps.)

---

## 5. The strongest indicator $i_{1,8,l}$ over the Doppler axis

The strongest coefficient of layer $l$ is the element
$k^{(2)}_{l,i^\star_l,f^\star_l,\tau^\star_l}$ with
$\tau^\star_l\in\{0,1\}$, $f^\star_l\in\{0,\dots,M_\upsilon-1\}$,
$i^\star_l\in\{0,\dots,2L-1\}$. After remapping $f^\star_l=0$, the search is
**among all $Q$ shifts of tap $f{=}0$**. With $I=2L\tau+i$ and, for $\upsilon=1$,
$\kappa^{(3)}_{1,I,0}=k^{(3)}_{1,i,0,\tau}$ (the flattened $f{=}0$ bitmap):

$$
i_{1,8,l} = \begin{cases}
\displaystyle\sum_{I=0}^{\,2L\tau^\star_1+i^\star_1}\kappa^{(3)}_{1,I,0}-1, & \upsilon=1\\[8pt]
2L\,\tau^\star_l + i^\star_l, & 1<\upsilon\le4
\end{cases}
\quad\in\{0,\dots,2LQ-1\}.
$$

Code (`encode_i18`/`decode_i18`): flatten the $(2L,Q)$ slice of tap 0 in
$I=2L\tau+i$ order.

* **Rank 1:** $i_{1,8}$ = cumulative count of nonzero $f{=}0$ bits up to
  $(i^\star,\tau^\star)$, minus 1. Field width is the per-layer nonzero $f{=}0$
  count (`validate_r18`: `n_i18 = i17[li,:,0,:].sum()`); the serializer still
  *packs* it in $\lceil\log_2 2LQ\rceil$ bits (a fixed-width encoding choice).
* **Rank > 1:** $i_{1,8}=2L\tau^\star+i^\star$ directly; range $[0, 2LQ)$.

`decode_i18` returns $(i^\star,\tau^\star)$. The validator checks the strongest
sits at $(\tau^\star, f{=}0, i^\star)$ with the fixed report values below.

### 5.1 What is and is not reported at the strongest entry

Per the clause, for $l=1,\dots,\upsilon$:

* $k^{(1)}_{l,\lfloor i^\star_l/L\rfloor}=15$,
  $k^{(2)}_{l,i^\star_l,0,\tau^\star_l}=7$,
  $k^{(3)}_{l,i^\star_l,0,\tau^\star_l}=1$, and
  $c_{l,i^\star_l,0,\tau^\star_l}=0$ — the strongest **reference amplitude,
  differential amplitude, and phase are NOT reported** (implied).
* The **other** reference amplitude
  $k^{(1)}_{l,(\lfloor i^\star_l/L\rfloor+1)\bmod 2}$ **is** reported.
* The $K^{NZ}-\upsilon$ remaining $k^{(2)}$ and $c$ (those with $k^{(3)}=1$ and not
  the strongest) are reported (3 bits / 4 bits each).
* The $2L M_\upsilon Q\upsilon - K^{NZ}$ zero-bitmap coefficients are not reported.

Code sets exactly `k1[li, p_star]=15`, `k2[li,tau*,0,i*]=7`, `c[li,tau*,0,i*]=0`,
and the serializer (`_pack_coefficients`) skips these implied values.

## 6. Reconstruction in code (`precoder`)

```python
W = zeros((N4, N3, P, rank))
for l in range(rank):
    taps = decode_taps(i16[l], N3, Mv, i15)
    Y = freq_basis(N3, taps).T              # (N3, Mv)
    Z = time_basis(N4, shifts(pmi, l)).T    # (N4, Q)
    x = layer_coefficients(pmi, l)          # (2L, Mv, Q)  = p1 * p2 * phi * bitmap
    ct = einsum("afq,tf,iq->ati", x, Y, Z)  # (2L, N3, N4)  <- freq AND time DFT
    gamma = sum(|ct|^2, axis=0)             # (N3, N4)
    w = [ B.T @ ct[:L] ; B.T @ ct[L:] ]     # over the flattened (N3,N4) axis
    W[:,:,:,l] = (w / sqrt(N1N2 * gamma)).transpose(...)
W /= sqrt(rank)
```

The single `einsum("afq,tf,iq->ati", ...)` applies the frequency DFT ($Y$) and the
temporal DFT ($Z$) at once, turning the $(2L, M_v, Q)$ core into a full
$(2L, N_3, N_4)$ response — the predicted precoder for every subband **and** every
future slot interval. The output `W` has a real first axis of length $N_4$
(every other codebook has length 1).

`precoder` calls `validate_r18` first (see [validate.py](../../src/nr_csi/codebooks/validate.py)):
shapes, ranges ($k^{(1)}\in[1,15]$, $k^{(2)}\in[0,7]$, $c\in[0,15]$, bitmap bool),
the $i_{1,10,l}\in[0,N_4-1)$ range, and the strongest-coefficient conventions.

## 7. Selection — genie-aided prediction (`select`)

R18's `select` receives the channel for the **$N_4$ future slot intervals**
(`H[slot=0..N4-1, t, rx, port]`) — the standard 3GPP upper-bound assumption of
*genie-aided prediction*. It fits the temporal bases to those intervals:

1. **Targets per interval.** `aligned_eigen_targets` for each of the $N_4$
   intervals; stack to $(N_4, N_3, P, v)$, flatten the time and frequency axes for
   a joint **group/beam selection** over all intervals.
2. **LS coefficients**, reshaped to $(v, N_4, N_3, 2L)$.
3. **Per layer:**
   * Phase-align to the most energetic beam (over time & frequency).
   * **FFT to delay** (`fft(C, axis=2)/N3`), select $M_v$ taps, share $i_{1,5}$.
   * **FFT to Doppler** (`fft(Csel, axis=1)/N4`). If $Q=2$, pick the strongest
     non-DC shift $n_4^{(1)}$ → $i_{1,10,l}$; keep shifts $\{0, n_4^{(1)}\}$.
   * Find the strongest coefficient among the $f{=}0$ shifts, normalize, build the
     bitmap (top-$K_0$, drop sub-floor with the $a_0/2$ amplitude floor, force
     strongest).
4. **Global budget** ($K^{NZ}\le 2K_0$, `_enforce_total_budget`) and **quantize**
   exactly as R16, now over the extra $\tau$ axis.

`select` honours `ri_restriction` (raises if the requested rank's bit is 0) and
checks the input has exactly $N_4$ slot intervals and $N_3$ frequency units.

Because the two transforms (delay, Doppler) are orthogonal DFTs, the fit is a
clean separable least-squares projection — the same structure as R16, one
dimension richer.

> **Note.** `select` assumes the gNB/UE already holds the *true* future channel
> ("genie"). The actual **CSI-prediction / measurement-window machinery** the spec
> wraps around this codebook is reporting/timing, not codebook math — see §10.

## 8. Overhead (`overhead_bits`) and bit-widths

```python
bits["i11"] = ceil(log2(O1*O2))              # q1,q2 (i1,1 / i1,2 group)
bits["i12"] = ceil(log2(C(N1*N2, L)))        # beam combination
# frequency (Algorithm 3, shared with R16):
#   N3 > 19:  i15 = ceil(log2(2*Mv)); i16 = v*ceil(log2(C(2Mv-1, Mv-1)))
#   N3 <= 19:                          i16 = v*ceil(log2(C(N3-1, Mv-1)))
bits["i17"] = v * 2L * Mv * Q                # bitmap carries the Q axis
bits["i18"] = v * ceil(log2(2L * Q))         # strongest over (beam, shift)
if N4 > 1:  bits["i110"] = v * ceil(log2(N4 - 1))   # 0 bits when N4 == 2
# coefficients (same shape as R16):
bits["i23"] = 4 * v                          # one reported k^(1) per layer (4b)
bits["i24"] = 3 * (K_nz - v)                 # reported k^(2): 3 bits each
bits["i25"] = 4 * (K_nz - v)                 # reported phases: 4 bits each
```

| index | meaning | width |
|------|---------|-------|
| $i_{1,1}/i_{1,2}$ (`i11`) | rotation $q_1,q_2$ | $\lceil\log_2 O_1O_2\rceil$ |
| $i_{1,2}$ (`i12`) | $L$-beam combination | $\lceil\log_2\binom{N_1N_2}{L}\rceil$ |
| $i_{1,5}$ | $M_{\text{initial}}$ ($N_3>19$) | $\lceil\log_2 2M_v\rceil$ |
| $i_{1,6,l}$ | tap combination | $\upsilon\lceil\log_2\binom{2M_v-1}{M_v-1}\rceil$ (or $\binom{N_3-1}{M_v-1}$) |
| $i_{1,7,l}$ | bitmap | $\upsilon\,2L\,M_v\,Q$ |
| $i_{1,8,l}$ | strongest indicator | $\upsilon\lceil\log_2 2LQ\rceil$ |
| $i_{1,10,l}$ | second-shift offset | $\upsilon\lceil\log_2(N_4-1)\rceil$ (**0 bits when $N_4=2$**) |
| $i_{2,3,l}$ | reference amp | $4\upsilon$ |
| $i_{2,4,l}$ | diff amps | $3(K^{NZ}-\upsilon)$ |
| $i_{2,5,l}$ | phases | $4(K^{NZ}-\upsilon)$ |

The cost over R16 is the $Q$ factor in the bitmap ($i_{1,7}$) and the per-layer
shift offset $i_{1,10}$. In return, **one** R18 report covers $N_4$ intervals,
where R16 would need $N_4$ separate reports. That is the fair comparison: the
overhead figures bill R15/R16 for $N_4$ reports and R18 for one (see
[metrics/overhead.py](../../src/nr_csi/metrics/overhead.py), `f2_comparison`).

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.** `overhead_bits` returns
> $\lceil\log_2 2LQ\rceil$ for $i_{1,8}$ in **all** ranks. The clause defines the
> rank-1 strongest indicator over the per-layer count of nonzero $f{=}0$ bits
> ($\sum_I\kappa^{(3)}_{1,I,0}$), so its true rank-1 width is
> $\lceil\log_2 K^{NZ}_{l,f=0}\rceil$, not the worst-case $\lceil\log_2 2LQ\rceil$.
> The wider field is a (safe, lossless) over-estimate, matching R16's convention.

## 9. The $N_4 = 1 \equiv$ R16 identity

When $N_4 = 1$ the PMI value corresponds to the codebook indices $i_1,i_2$ of
§5.2.2.2.5 and the precoder matrices for 1–4 layers come from Table 5.2.2.2.5-5.
Concretely: $Q = 1$, the temporal basis $Z$ is the scalar 1, $i_{1,10}$ is not
reported, and $K_0 = \lceil 2\beta L M_1\cdot 1\rceil = \lceil \beta\,2L\,M_1\rceil$
— exactly R16's $K_0$. The reconstruction reduces term-for-term to R16. The test
suite asserts **PMI-level equality** between `R18Type2Codebook(N4=1)` and
`R16Type2Codebook` on the same channel. This is the clean way to see R18 as
"R16 + a temporal axis."

## 10. Reporting / timing aspects the spec wraps around the codebook

§5.2.2.2.10 is the codebook; the *prediction* machinery lives in §5.2.1.4.2 and
§5.2.1.5.2. These shape what the codebook means but are **outside the codebook
math** — the code assumes genie-aided prediction (§7) and implements none of them:

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.** Slot-interval timing:
> the $N_4$ intervals each span $[l+jd, l+(j{+}1)d-1]$ for $j=0,\dots,N_4-1$, with
> time unit $d\in\{1,m\}$ (aperiodic) or $d=$ CSI-RS periodicity (P/SP), and the
> earliest interval starts at $l=n+\delta$ with slot offset
> $\delta\in\{-n_{CSI\_ref},0,1,2\}$ (`delta`). None of $d$, $\delta$,
> $n_{CSI\_ref}$, or the per-interval slot indexing is modelled; `select` simply
> takes $N_4$ pre-aligned channel slices.

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.** `TDCQI` time-domain CQI:
> $X\in\{1,2\}$ CQIs per report (modes `1-1`, `1-2`, `2`) keyed to specific slot
> intervals. The codebook produces precoders only; CQI is out of scope here.

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.** CPU-occupancy
> accounting $O_{CPU}$ for Doppler reports ($Y_1\cdot K$ aperiodic;
> $O_{CPU}=4$ for $N_4=1$ else $Y_2 N_4\ge4$ for P/SP) is a scheduling constraint,
> not modelled.

> 🚩 **STANDARDIZED — NOT IMPLEMENTED IN THIS CODEBASE.**
> `n1-n2-codebookSubsetRestriction-Doppler-r18` — the SD-beam subset restriction
> bitmap (configured as in §5.2.2.2.5, but with **only the `00` or `11` bit values
> of Table 5.2.2.2.5-6 configurable**). The constructor accepts no such bitmap and
> applies no SD restriction.

### 10.1 RI restriction — `typeII-Doppler-RI-Restriction-r18` (implemented)

A 4-bit bitmap $r_3 r_2 r_1 r_0$ ($r_0$ = LSB). When $r_i=0$, PMI/RI reporting may
not correspond to any precoder with $\upsilon=i+1$ layers; the UE shall not report
$\upsilon>4$. Implemented as `ri_restriction` (a 4-element bool array, indexed
`ri_restriction[rank-1]`); `select` raises if the requested rank is prohibited.
The coupled param-combo restriction ("8/9 forbidden when $r_i=1$ for any $i>1$")
is **[not implemented]** (see §2.1).

## 11. The compact / Tucker cross-check

[compact.py](../../src/nr_csi/codebooks/compact.py) gives two independent
expressions of the R18 precoder used to validate the spec-table reconstruction:

* **Kronecker form:** $\operatorname{vec}(W) = (\hat W_f \otimes \hat W_s)\, W_c\,
  \hat W_t^\top$ (`compact_r18`).
* **Tucker form:** $W = W_c \times_1 \hat W_s \times_2 \hat W_f \times_3 \hat W_t$
  (`tucker_r18`) — the core coefficient tensor $W_c$ contracted along the spatial,
  frequency, and temporal DFT factors.

Both must agree (direction-wise, ignoring the normalization the compact model sets
aside) with `precoder`. The three-factor Tucker view is the cleanest mental model
of the whole Type II family: **R15** = spatial factor only; **R16** = spatial +
frequency; **R18** = spatial + frequency + temporal.
